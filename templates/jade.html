Jade/Single Color Guide

: January 28th 2021    : 0   


I think we’ve all been there before; finally getting that brand new board game you’ve been eyeing up for months, ripping away the shrink-wrap, opening the box and what do you see?

Yea....In my dreams

…tons of unpainted miniatures. Well I guess it’s going to be another few months before this hits the table. But what if we had a method to paint these in one afternoon? Well nothing hyper detailed, but a simple nice looking single color paintjob. I mean, some games such as Rising Sun might even be better suited for a just a single color paint job anyways.

After all, there is a pragmatic reason to paint in a singular colors; it makes it obvious whose pieces belong to who when actually playing the game. In this guide I’ll teach you to paint figures in a single color along with a simple trick to make your figures have a glossy jade effect (pretty thematic for Rising Sun as well as the upcoming Great Wall!)

"Tan looks good on you General"

Required Materials
  • Paints
  • Brushes
  • Water
  • Dishwashing liquid /hand sanitizer
  • Protective Spray (Varnish)

We will be painting this figure from Rising Sun. Choose the color you want your figure to be painted in along with a lighter tone of that color or white if you don’t mind mixing. Black is also required as we’ll be mixing our own wash.


Step I

First step is start laying down the base coat in the darkest color you chose (not the black). Unless the paint isn’t sticking, there’s no need to prime. You may have to water down your paint so it spreads evenly and doesn’t clump on your figure but it wasn’t necessary here.

Spread your paint out evenly across your mini, don't let it get all bunched up anywhere

Blue plastic vs. blue paint

After getting every last centimeter of your mini with paint, time to let him dry. Meanwhile let’s make our wash.

Step II
Wash materials

A wash is basically a murky water that fills in the gaps of your mini, bringing out all the details and really making the figure pop. Because wash is relatively expensive, I prefer to mix my own which is what we will be doing here.

In a small container, mix 1 part of the base paint color you just applied to your mini, to 1 part black, to 2 parts water. Put a tiny drop of dishwashing liquid (preferably uncolored) or hand sanitizer into your mixture. This is to encourage your wash to run more smoothly into the crevices of your mini. Mix until its one homogenous liquid.

Left: 1:1 mix of black to base color Right: Small drop of hand sanitizer (which everyone definitely has right now)

After your mini has dried, apply the wash on with a clean paint brush. Watch your mini carefully as you should know immediately if your wash was mixed well. If done correctly, the crevices will fill with black whilst the mini still retains its original color:

Washing filling the crevices without distorting the color of mini

If done incorrect it can result in the two examples below:

Left: Too much water, wash is runny and not sticking Right: Too much paint(black in this case),thick and changing the mini's color

While the point of a wash is to add a dark color to crevices, you can overdo it by having too much wash drip and fill areas of your mini. With a clean brush give those areas a poke to soak up some wash. Also watch out for areas where the wash wasn’t even distributed. This is most commonly seen on smooth surfaces like the base of a figure.

Left: Too much wash piling up! Right: Wash not spread evenly

Give your figure time to fully dry.

Step III

Now we’re going to dry brush on one or two layers to bring out details. With your original base paint tone, mix in a little white or the lighter paint color to bring your base up a little. Or just use your lighter paint color (i.e. if your base was in dark rouge, use a lighter red color for your first dry brush layer). It’s up to you really what colors you want to add. The only thing I’d advise in terms of color selection is to go up in tone, darkest first all the way to lightest last in terms of layers of paint.

With that color ready, take a dry brush and dab the tips in paint. On a paper towel or newspaper, run your brush until most of the paint comes off. If you run your brush and you see streaks of paint, there’s still too much paint! Now brush away on your mini. A fast smothering motion, back and forth. You should start to see the details of your mini pop:

Dry brushing to add some character to your mini

After that you can add an additional even lighter toned layer if you want, it’s up to you. Same dry brushing method as before just applied for a shorter period of time on the parts of the mini that protrude the most (think sunlight hitting from above).

Step IV

And just like that the painting is done! Now for the varnish or protective spray. Unfortunately we won’t be varnishing the guy we just painted but this method was applied to my other already painted Rising Sun figures seen below.

Not varnished vs varnished

The protective spray is the key to the glossy jade effect or if you prefer just a regular matte finish. This guide assumes you purchased a matte finish varnish rather than a glossy finish, though I’d imagine the techniques are the same.

Give your can a good shake. In a well-ventilated area (outside is best) spray your mini in bursts from all angles. If you’re going for a matte finish, a minimal amount of spray will do as too much will cause glossiness…unless that glossiness is what you’re going for. To give that glossy jade effect, spray on a little more than you’re suppose to. Go by your eyes and judgment. Let your mini dry first and then inspect. How glossy is it? If it’s not enough than go for another layer of spray. Be aware though it’s easier to put this stuff on than to take it off, so be conservative with your spraying until you have a good handle on it.

Just pretend this is the same figure we've been painting this whole time

Left: minimal varnish Right: moderate amount (Glossy Jade effect starting to show)

Stabby Jr. and Stabby Sr.

And that’s it! This method is great because you can literally get through a hundred figures in just one afternoon. This is how I painted my clan figures in Rising Sun which accounts for about 80+ figures with all expansion packs included. I’ll likely be doing this for the Great Wall as well when that arrives. These look great and can even be thematic if you’re playing a game with an eastern theme!
